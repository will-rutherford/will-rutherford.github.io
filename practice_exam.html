Q1 — Depreciation & Disposal (12)

12Q1. Harbour Logistics Pty Ltd bought a forklift on 1 Sep 2025 for $180,000 (GST exclusive). Residual $20,000, useful life 8 years, total capacity 40,000 hours. Year-end 31 Dec.
(a) Straight-line depreciation for 2025 and 2026. (b) Diminishing-balance @ 20% for 2025 and 2026. (c) Units-of-production for 2025 (used 2,000 hours).
(d) A delivery van bought 1 Jan 2020 for $33,000 including 10% GST (SL, 6-yr life, residual $3,000) is sold on 31 Dec 2025 for $4,000 (ex-GST). Calculate the gain or loss on disposal.
(a) SL: (180,000 − 20,000)/8 = 20,000/yr. 2025 = 20,000 × 4/12 = $6,667; 2026 = $20,000.
(b) Diminishing @20%: 2025 = 180,000 × 20% × 4/12 = $12,000; 2026 = (180,000 − 12,000) × 20% = $33,600.
(c) Units: (180,000 − 20,000) × 2,000/40,000 = $8,000.
(d) Ex-GST cost = 33,000/1.1 = 30,000. Depreciable = 30,000 − 3,000 = 27,000; SL = 4,500/yr × 6 yrs (2020–2025) = 27,000 accumulated. Carrying amount = 30,000 − 27,000 = 3,000. Proceeds 4,000 − 3,000 = Gain $1,000.

Q2 — Bank Reconciliation + Allowance (10)

6Q2A. Reef Diving Pty Ltd's books show Cash at Bank of $5,240 (Dr). The bank statement shows $4,118. Bank charges $40; interest received $60; a customer's dishonoured cheque $310; outstanding deposits $1,520; a $500 receipt was recorded by the company as $608 (error $108 too high). Reconcile both sides.
Bank side: 4,118 + 1,520 outstanding deposits = $5,638.
Books side: 5,240 + 60 interest − 40 charges − 310 dishonoured − 108 error = $5,638 ✓
(The $608-vs-$500 error overstated cash by 108, so subtract it.)
4Q2B. Reef's opening allowance for doubtful debts is $6,200 Cr. During the year $4,100 of bad debts were written off. Year-end accounts receivable is $310,000. Bring the allowance to 2.5% of AR. Provide journal entries (narrations not required).
Write-off: Dr Allowance 4,100 / Cr Accounts receivable 4,100.
Required = 2.5% × 310,000 = 7,750. Balance after write-off = 6,200 − 4,100 = 2,100 Cr. Top-up = 7,750 − 2,100 = 5,650.
Dr Bad debts expense 5,650 / Cr Allowance for doubtful debts 5,650.

Q3 — Warranty + Share Issue + Dividends (8)

5Q3A. Summit Appliances Ltd offers a 12-month warranty. Opening warranty provision $48,000 Cr; during the year $51,200 of warranty work (labour) was performed; sales revenue $2,900,000; the warranty estimate is 2.0% of revenue. Provide the journal entries (narrations required).
Work performed: Dr Warranty provision 51,200 / Cr Wages payable 51,200 (warranty claims settled).
Balance after = 48,000 − 51,200 = 3,200 debit. Required = 2% × 2,900,000 = 58,000. Adjustment = 58,000 + 3,200 = 61,200.
Dr Warranty expense 61,200 / Cr Warranty provision 61,200 (year-end warranty provision).
3Q3B. On 1 Aug Quokka Ltd issued 3,000,000 shares at $1.50, fully payable on application (no over-subscription). On 20 Oct it declared a $0.10 dividend on its 9,000,000 shares, paid 5 Nov. Provide the journal entries.
Issue: Dr Cash 4,500,000 / Cr Share capital 4,500,000 (3M × $1.50).
Declaration: Dr Retained earnings 900,000 / Cr Dividends payable 900,000 (9M × $0.10).
Payment: Dr Dividends payable 900,000 / Cr Cash 900,000.

Q6 — Ratios + Commentary (9)

9Q6. Boulder Building Ltd. Current assets $240,000 (incl. inventory $90,000, prepaid $10,000); current liabilities $80,000. Net credit sales $730,000 (sales returns $30,000 already deducted to get this); COGS $420,000. Average AR $58,000; average inventory $70,000. Net operating cash $96,000; capital expenditure $60,000.
(a) Current ratio; quick ratio; average collection period; average days in inventory; free cash flow.
(b) Last year the average collection period was 22 days. Comment on the change and suggest one cause.
(a)
Current ratio = 240,000 / 80,000 = 3.0.
Quick ratio = (240,000 − 90,000 − 10,000) / 80,000 = 140,000/80,000 = 1.75.
Receivables turnover = 730,000 / 58,000 = 12.59× ⇒ Avg collection period = 365 / 12.59 = 29.0 days.
Inventory turnover = 420,000 / 70,000 = 6.0× ⇒ Avg days in inventory = 365 / 6.0 = 60.8 days.
Free cash flow = 96,000 − 60,000 = $36,000.
(b) The collection period rose from 22 to 29 days — a deterioration: customers are taking ~7 days longer to pay. Likely causes: a more lax credit policy, weaker collection follow-up, relaxed credit terms to win sales, or customer disputes. (Any one well-explained cause earns the mark.)
