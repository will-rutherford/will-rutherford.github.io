ACCT1110 — Ratio Reference Sheet

Formulae are GIVEN in the exam — this is for practice + knowing what each measures.

In the exam you mainly need to (1) pick the right numbers and (2) write the commentary. (← back to hub) · Watch: "average" = (open+close)/2; "net credit sales" = sales − returns; capex = ΔPPE + depreciation.

Liquidity — can it pay short-term debts?

RatioFormulaMeasures
Current ratioCurrent assets / Current liabilitiesShort-term assets covering short-term debts. >1 = positive working capital; very high may mean idle assets.
Quick (acid-test) ratio(Current assets − inventory − prepaid) / CLCan pay CL without selling inventory. Benchmark ≈ 1; <1 = concern.
Current cash debt coverageNet operating cash / Avg current liabilitiesLiquidity from operating cash. Below 0.40 warrants investigation.
Receivables turnoverNet credit sales / Avg accounts receivableHow many times receivables collected per year. Higher = faster collection.
Average collection period365 / Receivables turnoverDays to collect receivables. Lower is better.
Inventory turnoverCOGS / Avg inventoryTimes inventory sold per year.
Average days in inventory365 / Inventory turnoverDays stock is held. Rising = slower-moving / obsolescence risk.

Solvency — can it survive long-term?

RatioFormulaMeasures
Debt to assetsTotal liabilities / Total assets% of assets financed by debt (leverage).
Times interest earnedEBIT / Interest expenseAbility to meet interest from profit. Rule of thumb 3–4×; <1 = trouble.
Cash debt coverageNet operating cash / Avg total liabilitiesRepay all debt from operating cash. Below 0.20 warrants investigation.
Free cash flowNet operating cash − Capital expenditureDiscretionary cash for dividends/expansion.
Capital expenditure ratioNet operating cash / Capital expenditureWhether operations fund asset investment.

Profitability — how well does it generate profit?

RatioFormulaMeasures
Gross profit rate(Net sales − COGS) / Net salesGross margin per sales dollar. Falling = price cuts / rising COGS.
Profit marginProfit after tax / Net salesNet profit per sales dollar.
Operating expenses to salesOperating expenses / Net salesCost to support each sales dollar.
Return on assets (ROA)Profit after tax / Avg total assetsReturn on assets controlled. ~5–20%.
Return on equity (ROE)Profit after tax / Avg shareholders' equityReturn on owners' investment. ~5–20%.
Asset turnoverNet sales / Avg total assetsSales generated per asset dollar.
Cash return on salesNet operating cash / Net salesCash version of profit margin.
Earnings per share (EPS)Profit after tax / Weighted avg ordinary sharesProfit per ordinary share.
Price-earnings (P/E)Market price per share / EPSPrice investors pay per $1 of earnings.
Cash dividend payoutDividends / Profit% of profit paid to shareholders.

PPE / asset-management

RatioFormulaMeasures
Average useful life of PPEAvg cost of PPE / Depreciation expenseEstimated total life of asset base.
Average age of PPEAccumulated depreciation / Depreciation expenseHow far through their life assets are.
Credit risk ratioAllowance for doubtful debts / Accounts receivableProportion of receivables expected uncollectible.
Cash adequacy ratioCash / Average daily cash expensesDays of expenses cash on hand can cover.

Commentary recipe: (1) state direction (improved/declined, from X to Y), (2) interpret what it means, (3) give a plausible cause or caveat. That third sentence is where the marks are.
